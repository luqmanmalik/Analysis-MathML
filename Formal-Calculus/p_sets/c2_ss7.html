We'll assume that the statement holds for all $p_i \leq p$ where $p_i \in \mathbb{N}.$ The binomial theorem yields the equation $$(k+1)^{p+1}-k^{p+1}=(p+1)k^p+\lambda_0 k^{p-1}+\lambda_1 k^{p-2}+\cdots+\lambda_m k^1+1$$ Writing this formula for $k=1,\dots,n$ and adding, we obtain $$ \begin{array}{rcl} 2^{p+1}-1^{p+1} &=& (p+1) \cdot 1^p+\lambda_0 \cdot 1^{p-1}+\lambda_1 \cdot 1^{p-2}+\cdots+\lambda_m \cdot 1^1+1 \\ 3^{p+1}-2^{p+1} &=& (p+1) \cdot 2^p+\lambda_0 \cdot 2^{p-1}+\lambda_1 \cdot 2^{p-2}+\cdots+\lambda_m \cdot 2^1+1 \\ \ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ (n+1)^{p+1}-n^{p+1} & = & (p+1) \cdot n^p+\lambda_0 \cdot n^{p-1} +\lambda_1 \cdot n^{p-2}+\cdots+\lambda_m \cdot n^1+1 \\ \hline (n+1)^{p+1}-1^{\color{transparent}{p+1}} &=& (p+1)\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^n k^p+\lambda_0\sum_{k=1}^n k^{p-1}+\cdots+\lambda_m\sum_{k=1}^n k^1+n. \\ &\Downarrow& \\ \dfrac{(n+1)^{p+1}}{p+1} &=& \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^n k^p+\lambda_i\sum_{k=1}^n k^r, \ \ \ \ \ \text{for} \ i \in \mathbb{N} \ \text{and} \ r < p. \end{array} $$ By assumption, we can write each $\displaystyle T=\lambda_i\sum_{k=1}^nk^r$ as an expression involving powers $n^s$ with $s \leq p.$ It follows that

$\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^nk^p=\frac{(n+1)^{p+1}}{p+1}+T, \ \text{where} \ \forall n^s \ \text{composing} \ T, \ s < p+1. \ \square$
