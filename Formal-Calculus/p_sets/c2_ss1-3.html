$\text{Clearly} \ \displaystyle{1 \choose 1} \ \text{is a natural number. Suppose that} \ \displaystyle {n \choose p} \ \text{is a natural number}$
$\text{for all} \ p \leq n.\ \text{Since} \ 3.\text{(a) is established, and in particular}$ $$ \displaystyle {n+1 \choose p} = {n \choose p-1} + {n \choose p} \ \ \ \ \ \ \text{for} \ p \leq n, $$ $\text{it follows that} \ \displaystyle{n+1 \choose p} \ \text{is a natural number for all} \ p \leq n, \ \text{while} \ {n+1 \choose n+1}$
$\displaystyle \text{is also a natural number. So} \ {n+1 \choose p} \ \text{is a natural number}$
$ \text{for all} \ p \leq n+1. \ \ \square$
