01. Prove the following:

$ \begin{align} &\text{(i)} \ \text{If} \ ax=a \ \text{for some number} \ a \neq 0, \ \text{then} \ x=1. \\ &\text{(ii)} \ x^2-y^2 = (x-y)(x+y). \\ &\text{(iii)} \ \text{If} \ x^2=y^2, \ \text{then} \ x=y \ \text{or} \ x=-y. \\ &\text{(iv)} \ x^3-y^3 = (x-y)(x^2 + xy + y^2). \\ &\text{(v)} \ x^n-y^n = (x-y)(x^{n-1} + x^{n-2}y + \dots + xy^{n-2} + y^{n-1}). \end{align} $

02. What is wrong with the following "proof"? Let $x=y.$ Then $$ \require{cancel} \begin{equation} \begin{split} x^2 &= xy, \\ x^2-y^2 &= xy-y^2, \\ (x+y)(x-y) &= y(x-y), \\ x+y &= y, \ \ \ \ \color{grey}{\text{Err} \ \ \cancel{0 \vert a}} \\ 2y &= y, \\ 2 &= 1. \\ & \ \square \end{split} \end{equation} $$
