THEOREM The product of two numbers $a$ and $b$ is positive when $a,b > 0$ or $a,b < 0.$
PROOF The first condition, i.e., $a,b>0$ is trivial, and follows from P12–closure under multiplication. The second can be proved as follows:

$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} \color{grey}{\text{lemma:}} \ (-a) \cdot b + a \cdot b &= [(-a)+a] \cdot b \\ &= 0 \cdot b = 0. \\ & \ \ \downarrow \\ -(a \cdot b) \ \ & \ \ \ \ \ \ -(a \cdot b) \\ & \ \ \downarrow \\ (-a) \cdot b \ \ &= \ -(a \cdot b). \\ & \ {\Large\circ} \end{split} \end{equation} $ $ \begin{equation} \begin{split} \color{grey}{\text{corrollary:}} \ (-a) \cdot (-b) + [-(a \cdot b )] &= (-a) \cdot (-b) + (-a) \cdot b \\ &= (-a)[(-b)+b] \cdot b = 0. \\ & \ \ \downarrow \\ + (a \cdot b) \ \ & \ \ \ \ \ \ +(a \cdot b) \\ & \ \ \downarrow \\ (-a) \cdot (-b) \ \ &= \ \ a \cdot b. \\ & \ \square \end{split} \end{equation} $
