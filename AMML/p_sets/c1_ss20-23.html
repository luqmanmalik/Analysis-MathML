20. By theorem 1, we know that $\forall x \in \mathbb{R}, \ \lvert x+y \rvert \leq \lvert x \rvert - \lvert y \rvert.$ Therefore, if $$\lvert x-x_0 \rvert < \frac{\varepsilon}{2} \ \ \ \ \ \ \text{and} \ \ \ \ \ \ \lvert y-y_0 \rvert < \frac{\varepsilon}{2},$$ then
$$ \begin{align} \lvert x-x_0 \rvert + \lvert y-y_0 \rvert &< \varepsilon \\ \lvert (x-x_0) + (y-y_0) \rvert &< \varepsilon \ \succ \text{pg.12} \\ \lvert (x+y) - (x_0+y_0) \rvert &< \varepsilon \\ &\ \square \end{align} $$ $$ \begin{align} \lvert x-x_0 \rvert + \lvert y-y_0 \rvert &< \varepsilon \\ \lvert (x-x_0) - (y-y_0) \rvert &< \varepsilon \ \succ \text{pg.12} \\ \lvert (x-y) - (x_0-y_0) \rvert &< \varepsilon \\ &\ \square \end{align} $$
21. By problem 12(v) / inverse triangle inequality:

$\lvert x \rvert - \lvert x_0 \rvert \leq \lvert x-x_0 \rvert < 1 \ \Rightarrow \ \lvert x \rvert < 1+\vert x_0 \rvert,$

therefore $$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} \lvert xy-x_0y_0 \rvert \\ =\lvert x(y-y_0)+y_0(x-x_0) \rvert &\leq \lvert x(y-y_0) \rvert + \lvert y_0(x-x_0) \rvert \\ &\leq \lvert x \rvert \lvert y-y_0 \rvert + \lvert y_0 \rvert \lvert x-x_0 \rvert \ \succ \ 12(i) \\ &< \bigg(\color{grey}{\cancel{1+\vert x_0 \rvert}} \bigg)\bigg[\frac{\varepsilon}{2(\color{grey}{\cancel{\lvert x_0 \rvert +1)}}}\bigg]+\vert y_0 \rvert \bigg[\frac{\varepsilon}{2(\lvert y_0 \rvert +1)}\bigg] \\ &< \frac{\varepsilon}{2} + \bigg(\frac{\lvert y_0 \rvert}{\lvert y_0 \rvert+1}\bigg)\bigg(\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\bigg) \\ &< \frac{\varepsilon}{2} + \frac{\varepsilon}{2} = \varepsilon. \\ &\ \square \end{split} \end{equation} $$
22. By the triangle inequality theorem, we have

$\lvert y_0 \rvert = \lvert (y_0 - y) + y \rvert \leq \lvert y - y_0 \rvert + \lvert y \rvert.$

This, taken with the given condition $\lvert y - y_0 \rvert < \lvert y_0 \lvert| / 2$ suggests that $$ \frac{\lvert y_0 \rvert}{2} = \lvert y_0 \rvert - \frac{\lvert y_0 \rvert}{2} < \lvert y_0 \rvert - \lvert y - y_0 \rvert \leq \lvert y \rvert. $$ Now, we apply the condition $\lvert y - y_0 \rvert < \dfrac{\varepsilon \lvert y_0 \rvert^2}{2}$ to the numerator in the following expression: $$ \Bigg\lvert \frac{1}{y} - \frac{1}{y_0} \Bigg\rvert = \frac{\lvert y - y_0 \rvert}{\lvert y \rvert\lvert y_0 \rvert} < \dfrac{\lvert y - y_0 \rvert}{\dfrac{\lvert y_0 \rvert\lvert y_0 \rvert}{2}} < \frac{\varepsilon\lvert y_0 \rvert^2}{2}\bigg/\frac{\lvert y_0 \rvert^2}{2} = \varepsilon $$ from which we obtain our desired result. $\square$
